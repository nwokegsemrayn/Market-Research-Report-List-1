<p><p>Optical Fiber Current Sensors (OFCS) utilize the principles of fiber optics to measure electrical currents, offering advantages like immunity to electromagnetic interference and high sensitivity. The market for OFCS is primarily divided into two types: AC and DC. AC OFCSs detect alternating currents, which fluctuate over time, while DC OFCSs measure direct currents that remain constant. Both markets cater to different applications, with AC sensors often used in power distribution and renewable energy systems, and DC sensors finding applications in electronics and battery monitoring.</p></p>
